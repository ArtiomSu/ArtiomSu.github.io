What is Arduino Custom Multilayer Mouse?

As you can see by the photos above this custom mouse uses a joystick to move the cursor and 4 additional buttons for other actions. I used cherry MX blues as I have some left over from my keyboard build

The Multilayer aspect allows you to have 3 different layers based on the state of the 4th key. This is similiar to QMK if you are familiar with that. Currently there are 3 layers see table bellow

Also pressing the joystick button will reset the mouse to defaults this is handy if you want to quickly turn off the autoclickers

Layer Button 1 Button 2 Button 3 Button 4
Normal left click right click middle click hold=Shifted, press=Special
Shifted browser back browser forward toggle auto clicker*
Special scroll up scroll down toggle afk auto clicker* hold=Shifted, press=Normal
toggle auto clicker* when enabled buttons 1-3 in Normal layer will repeat when held down for around 30 clicks per second
toggle afk auto clicker* when enabled the left click will keep on spamming non stop at around 30 clicks per second

Note: Button 1 is the closest button to the joystick

Basically if you hold button 4 you enter shifted layer. And if you press button 4 you will toggle between Special layer and Normal layer. If you connected an led to pin 4 if will blink while in Special layer.

Auto Clicking makes more sense if you watch the youtube video linked above and if you are familiar with software auto clickers this should make sense to you.

Soldering Iron, Solder wire

Hot Glue Gun

Whatever tools needed to make the housing

pc with arduino studio installed (free)

Housing/Shell

I build the housing from a toilet paper roll and cardboard hot glued together. Then I covered the whole thing with plastic using my 3d pen to give is structure. Ideally this should be 3d printed but I don't have a 3d printer so this is how it is.

If you decide to make your own make sure you double check that the switches fit snugly otherwise you might have hot glue them in.

Assembly

For the assembly I will refer to the photos above the text.

We will start of by desoldering the pins on the joystick as otherwise it wont fit in the housing. Keep the desoldered pins as we will use them later on to connect all of the switches to ground.

During the soldering I have made a mistake and ripped some of the solder points from the pcb so I followed the traces and soldered directly to the points as you can see in the first photo

Ideally all of your pins should be soldered like in the second photo where I have only two pins soldered to the solder points.

After soldering the five wires you can hotglue the bottom of the pcb to prevent shorts and also to keep all the wires together.

Joystick Pin out

Connection on the Joystick Wire Colour Connection on the Arduino
GND brown GND
+5V orange VCC
VRx red A0
VRy yellow A1
SW green 9

You can now insert the joystick into the housing. Dont worry about the switches just yet you can actually remove them from the housing as we will be soldering wires onto them later.

I used hot glue to keep the joystick in place as can be seen in photos 1 and 2.

I then covered the open space with my 3d pen for a more complete look.

Don't worry about getting the joystick in backwards but if you want to follow me I made sure that the pins on the joystick are facing towards the back. If you have yours facing away you can simply swap the joystick x,y variables in code later on.

We will now solder wires to the switches

One side of the switch will go to ground on the arduino, this is located near the vcc on mine. And the other side will go to an io pin on the arduino I used pins 5-8 as pin 9 is the joystick button

Since we have a lot of wires going to ground we will join them using the desoldered pins from the joystick as seen in the third photo. Strip a wire and solder it to all of the pins to connect them all

Insert all of the switched though the holes and you can push them in place

Next connect one side of the 4 switches together to the desoldered pins from the joystick discussed in the last paragraph. This can be seen in the third photo. Connect this to the ground pin on the arduino

Now you can connect the other side of the switches to pins 5-8 on the arduino. You can change these in code later on but if you want to follow exactly this is how I did it

pin 7 = switch 1, pin 8 = switch 2, pin 6 = switch 3 and pin 5 = switch 4.

You can now hot glue the arduino in place. A good trick is plug the arduino through the hole into the usb port and hot glue while plugged in. This will ensure a perfect connection.

At this point I added an indicator led for letting me know If I'm using the Special layer. If you want an led you can connect it to pin 4 and a spare ground pin on the arduino.

The Build is now complete take a look at the Code Guide to flash the firmware and edit the config.
